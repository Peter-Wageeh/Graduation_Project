About Us

Project Name: Optimization and Manufacturing of Fully Composite UAV Powered by Solar Energy

Department: Aerospace & Aeronautical Department

Faculty: Faculty of Engineering

University: Cairo University

The Project is divided into two complementary sub-phases:

  1. Design and manufacturing of a composite structure-frame, to achieve weight reduction, and to sustain larger loads.
  2. Developing a solar power system for the new composite frame, to sustain longer flight endurance to achieve the required missions.

Solar-electrically powered Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) promise remarkable increase in flight endurance over purely-electrically or gas-powered aircraft. As the solar-powered UAV uses excess solar energy gathered during the day to recharge its batteries.

This capability makes solar-powered UAVs great candidates for applications in which data needs to be collected or distributed either continuously or on a large scale, industrial and agricultural sensing, mapping, Large-scale disaster relief support missions, meteorological surveys in remote areas and continuous border patrol are great examples. 4 The study’s Third phase ends by, starting planning the Fourth phase in the following year, which will introduce an auto-pilot system for the solar-UAV.

Composite materials are combinations of two or more different materials, it gives superior quality than its constituents. composite materials can be used not only for structural applications, but also in various applications such as aerospace, automobiles, etc. fiber reinforced plastic materials are widely used in various engineering industries because of their superior performance.so we used the composite material especially carbon fiber in our project.

The main object of this project is to design and analysis structure for UAV Flying wing which already have its aerodynamics characteristics in previous project 2010. The main stress on a beam in wing which carry all bending on the UAV and our point is to design and manufacture the best cross-section also the right number of plies from composite materials, which we made in one mathematical model analytically and numerically with its implementation for the first time in our department “Aerospace Engineering”.

This study is considered the third phase of the design and manufacturing of our proposed UAV, where the first phase was in 2010, considered only the design and manufacturing of a balsa-frame, powered by lithium-batteries. The first phase yielded an UAV, that ranked first in three different competitions, while the second phase was in 2019 but it didn’t complete because of the spread of corona virus in the countries.
